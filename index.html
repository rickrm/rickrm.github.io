Projects

BCSS Wildcats iOS App

I programmed an IOS mobile application called BCSS Wildcats to improve the lives of the student body. I worked with the principals and teachers at Burnaby Central Secondary school to design and develop the app over the span of a year. Then I went through product testing with a group of students, in hopes of making each component perfect. After the launch of my app, there are now over 300 students using it.

Joke Lab Android App

A fun project that I developed to explore Android development and design, while also sharing my own enjoyment of jokes, especially dad jokes!

Forest Fire Research Drone

For our Software Engineering course, my group and I decided to create a drone that would help combat the increasingly dangerous growth of forest fires. The drone has autopilot capabilities that allows it to go around forest fires and generate a heatmap to better understand the landscape and conditions of forest fires in a safe way. I mainly worked on developing the heat map generator using Python

TEDxYouth@DeerLake Website

About Me

I currently attend the Software Engineering program at the University of Waterloo. Having been diagnosed with a brain tumour as a child, I faced many hardships and tough days. Since then, I realized that I wanted to provide help and support for those in unfortunate situations through each line of code I write and each idea I construct. My goal is to resolve some of world's biggest problems that harms the lives of many, such as pollution and poverty.
